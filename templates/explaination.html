LESSON (1):

What is trigonometry?

Trigonometry is a branch of mathematics that studies relationships involving lengths and angles of triangles.

Angle:

The space (usually measured in degrees) between two intersecting lines or surfaces at or close to the point where they meet.

Triangle identities:

There are 3 angles in a triangle (tip: tri-angle, tri = 3). The sum of the angles in a triangle is 180 degrees.
There are types of triangles:
1. By angles:

Obtuse triangle: one of the angles is over 90 degrees (only one angle is greater than 90 degrees, and the side that is opposite to the 90 degrees is called a Hypotenuse). Acute triangle: all three angles are less than 90 degrees. Right triangle: one of the angles is 90 degrees (if this is the case, there can only be one right angle (90)), TIP: the side that is opposite to the 90 degrees is called a hypotenuse.

2. By side lengths:

Equilateral triangle: All the sides have equal lengths and all the angles are 60 degrees

b. Isosceles triangle: Two sides are of equal lengths and two of the angles are equal.
c. Scalene triangle : all sides are of different lengths, and all angles are different.
